RFID and NFC Attacks

RFID (Radio-frequency identification)
- this technology is everywhere
- access badges
- inventory/assembly line tracking
- pet/animal identification
- anything that needs to be tracked

- this is radar technology
- radio energy is transmitted to the tag
- RF powers the tag, ID is transmitted back
- this is bidirectional communication
- some tag formats can be active/powered


- susceptible to both physical and electronic attacks
- prevents the theft of electronic items


RFID Attacks
- data capture
- view communication
- replay attack

- try spoofing the reader
- write your own data to the RF tag

- denial of service is possible
- susceptible to signal jamming

- decrypt communication
- many default keys can be found with Google


Near Field Communication
- two-way wireless communication
- builds on RFID, which was one-way

- used to make wireless payments
- built into most bank cards, someone with a skimmer could steal your info
- prevent skimming by placing your card into an aluminum pouch or wallet

- payment systems
- Google wallet and MasterCard partnership

- can be used as a bootstrap for other wireless technologies
- NFC can help with bluetooth pairing

- access tokens and identity cards
- short range with encryption support


NFC Security Concerns
- susceptible to remote capture
- wireless data
- can be captured from up to 10 meters

- frequency jamming
- denial of service

- relay/replay attacks
- susceptible to man in the middle attacks

- loss of RFC device control
- stolen/lost phone
