Wireless Replay Attacks

Wired vs. Wireless Replay
- similar to a wired replay attack
- wireless doesn't change those attacks

- wireless adds some additional capabilities
- this is a big concern for security professionals

- its much easier to capture data wirelessly
- hotspots are generally easily exploitable
- similar to tuning in to a radio station


Cracking WEP
- WEP: Wired Equivalent Privacy
- a broken security protocol
- could not stop the replay of 802.11 packets

- ARP request replay attack
- cracking WEP requires thousands of initialization vector packets
- you can wait all day to collect IV information
- or you can replay a ton of ARPs and collect the IV packets

- once you have thousands of packets you can crack WEP in seconds


WEP IV Attack
- an initialization vector is an arbitrary number that can be used along with a secret key for data encryption

- use of an iv prevents repetition in data encryption
- makes dictionary attacks less effective

- once an attacker learns the plain text of one packet, they can compute the RC4 key stream generated by the IV used
