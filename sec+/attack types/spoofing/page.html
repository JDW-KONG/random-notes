Spoofing
- pretend to be something you aren't
- fake web server, fake DNS server, etc.

- email address spoofing
- the sending email address isn't really the sender

- caller id spoofing
- the incoming call information is completely fake

- man in the middle attacks
- arp spoofing
- the person in the middle of the conversation pretends to be both parties


MAC Spoofing
- the theft of the MAC address of another networked device
- can be used to gain access to the network

- your ethernet device has a MAC address
- a unique burned in address
- most drivers allow you to change this


- changing the MAC address can be legitimate
- your internet provider may expect a certain MAC address
- certain applications require a particular MAC address

- it might not be legitimate
- circumvent MAC-based ACLs
- can be used to fake out a wireless address filter

- very difficult to detect
- how do you know it's not the original address?


IP Address Spoofing
- the creation of internet protocol packets with a false source IP address
- can hide the identity of the sender
- can impersonate another computing system so that they cannot be traced when they carry out a malicious attack

- take someone else's IP address
- spoof the ip of an actual device
  • - pretend to be someone and somewhere you are not

- can be legit
- when load balancing or load testing

- may not be legit
- ARP poisoning
- DNS amplification / DDoS

- easier to identify than MAC address spoofing
- apply rules to prevent invalid traffic
- enable switch security
