Social Engineering
- attack types are constantly changing
- you never know what they'll use next

- may involve multiple people
- multiple organizations as well
- there are ties connecting many organizations

- may be in person or electronic
- phone calls from aggressive customers
- emailed funeral notifications of a friend or associate


Social Engineering Principles
- Authority
- the social engineer appears to be in charge
- calls from helpdesk/CEO/police/etc.
  • - impersonating an authority figure to solicit information from a victim

- Intimidation
- there will be negative consequences if you don't comply
- pretending to be someone of high authority and threatening the victim with consequences if they don't comply
- victims are made to believe they have no other choice

- Consensus
- convince based on what's normally accepted
- complying with demands because others appear to have done the same
- also known as social proof

- Scarcity
- the situation will not be this way for long
- setting a fake time limit or deadline that has to be met

- Urgency
- works alongside scarcity
- act quickly, don't think
- instills a sense of urgency and panic in the victim, causing them to comply amidst the confusion

- Familiarity/Liking
- someone you know, we have common friends
  • - the attacker makes himself familar to the victim
  • - the attacker is eventually trusted and the attack begins

- Trust
- someone who is safe
- I'm from IT and I'm here to help
