Denial of Service
- victim's machine or network is flooded with a high volume of requests
- this prevents other hosts from using the service

- common method is to use a SYN flood attack
- first two parts of the three way handshake take place
- this leaves your PC in a state of limbo
- DDoS attacks often involve a high volume of these

- forcing a service to fail
- overload the service

- take advantages of a design failure or vulnerability
- keep your systems patched

- causing a system to be unavailable for a competitive advantage

- create a smokescreen for some other exploit
- precursor to a DNS spoofing attack

- doesn't have to be complicated


A Friendly DoS
- unintentional DoSing

- network DoS
- layer 2 loop without STP

- Bandwidth DoS
- downloading multiple gigabyte linux distros over a DSL connection


Distributed Denial of Service
- a botnet is set up to flood a victim's system with an enormous amount of traffic
- this causes the system to go down or malfunctiion

- launch an army of computers to bring down a service
- use all the bandwidth or resources - traffic spike

- this is why the bad guys have botnets
- droves of computers at their command
- at peak, Zeus botnet infected over 3.6 million PCs
- coordinated attack

- asymmetric threat
- the attacker may have fewer resources than the victim

- stateful firewalls can be set up to prevent DDoS attacks from gaining access to your network


DDoS Amplification
- a DNS amplification
- DDoS attack in which the attacker exploits vulnerabilities in DNS servers to turn small queries into much larger payloads
- these payloads are used to bring down a victim's servers

- turn your small attack into a big attack
- often reflected off another device or service

- an increasingly common DDoS technique
- turn internet services against the victim

- uses protocols with few, if any, authentication checks
- NTP, DNS, ICMP
- a common example of protocol abuse
