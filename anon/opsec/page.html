Operational Security
- habits and behaviors you perform to enforce good security

- most underrated security control

- privacy: identity is known, but activities are hidden
- anonymity: identity is hidden, but activities may be seen
- pseudo-anonymity: using an alias, but concealing your true identity

- there is not a magic way to trace people through Tor
- capitalize on human error to de-anonymize targets
- basic OPSEC failures are usually what get people caught
- sound OPSEC is required from the very beginning to maintain privacy and anonymity


Identities, Pseudonyms, and Aliases
- consider the risks of what you post online
- how might your personal opinions and activities affect your life?
- the internet does not forget

- develop an identity strategy

- 6 main strategies
  • - open strategy
  • - using your true identity and being open and honest about everything
  • - avoidance
  • - avoiding social media and interacting with others online
  • - audience
  • - separate aliases for different audiences
  • - content
  • - create content that matches a consistent appearance to all people
  • - compartmentalization
  • - use one or more identities online
  • - required
  • - custom
  • - best strategy that combines elements from all strategies

10 Rules of OPSEC
- Hacker OPSEC
  • - https://grugq.github.io/
- keep your mouth shut
  • - never reveal details about anything
  • - keep the technologies you use secret
  • - never be explicit

- trust no one
  • - info should be given out on a need to know basis only
  • - no co-conspirators
  • - reveal very little about yourself
  • - watch for a change in the habits of others
  • - operate alone
- never contaminate identities
  • - do not share anything between aliases
    • - email addresses
    • - accounts
    • - friends
    • - ip addresses
    • - cookies
    • - browsers
    • - email clients
    • - operating systems
    • - passwords
  • - don't post to multiple aliases at once

- never log into an account using Tor if you have already logged in to it without Tor
- leave your mobile phone at home when using an offsite internet connection

- don't use an internet connection connected to your real identity

- use one phone for each identity
- do not contact one aliases contacts with the phone of a different alias

- be uninteresting
- make everything as uninteresting as possible
- hide your knowledge and conceal your use of security controls
- don't maintain accounts unless absolutely necessary
- don't draw the attention of a well resourced adversary
- establish a believable, average alias
- don't use an alias longer than you have to

- be actively paranoid
- always consider all the angles
- spend time thinking from your adversary's perspectives
- be aware at all times
- plan to be arrested
- use a VPN kill switch
- don't use wireless peripherals
- switch off all unused electronic devices
- never leave your devices unlocked and attended
- always power down your devices
- don't mention anything about security or hacking

- know your limitations
- stick with tech you understand and can effectively implement

- minimize info
  • - no logs == no crime
- do not leave evidence
- send as little info as possible in communications
- don't send cleartext messages, encrypt everything
- do not leave a money trail

- be professional
- treat this with the seriousness it requires
- treat it as a business

- employ anti-profiling
- avoid revealing personal info about yourself
- do not tell stories about yourself
- remain an enigma
- do not reveal anything that can reveal your location or time zone
- avoid keeping regular hours
  • - provide misinformation
- protect your assets
  • - do not transmit information without end to end encryption
